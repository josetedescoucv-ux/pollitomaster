# Pollito Master Roadmap

## Phase 0 — Foundation

**Goal**
Create the stable foundation for a desktop-first productivity platform.

**User value**
Users get a polished, dependable workspace that feels purposeful and ready for daily use.

**Main features**
- Project setup and development environment
- Core architecture and product structure
- Desktop shell and responsive workspace layout
- Theme system and visual language
- Navigation model for primary product areas

**Dependencies**
- Product direction and design principles
- Architecture decisions
- Design system foundation

**Completion criteria**
- The desktop experience is usable as a stable shell
- Navigation between core areas is clear
- The interface feels coherent and professional
- The foundation is ready for feature development

**Estimated complexity**
Medium

---

## Phase 1 — Planner MVP

**Goal**
Deliver the first usable planning experience centered on weekly organization.

**User value**
Users can plan their week, structure their day, and manage events without friction.

**Main features**
- Week planner
- Vertical day columns
- Event cards
- Color categories
- Event editor
- Manual time entry
- Keyboard shortcuts

**Dependencies**
- Core planning data model
- Desktop shell and navigation
- Interaction design for planning views
- Keyboard and accessibility considerations

**Completion criteria**
- Users can create and edit planned events
- The planner is usable for weekly scheduling
- The experience feels fast and readable
- Core interactions are understandable without training

**Estimated complexity**
High

---

## Phase 2 — Smart Planning

**Goal**
Make planning more powerful and adaptable through recurring patterns and faster discovery.

**User value**
Users save time by reusing plans, locating events quickly, and refining what they see.

**Main features**
- Recurring events
- Templates
- Drag and drop
- Search
- Filters

**Dependencies**
- Planner MVP
- Event and category model refinement
- Interaction patterns for editing and organizing plans

**Completion criteria**
- Recurring events can be created and managed reliably
- Templates provide a useful shortcut for repeated planning needs
- Users can reorganize and find content quickly
- The planner remains clear even as it grows more capable

**Estimated complexity**
High

---

## Phase 3 — Desktop Productivity

**Goal**
Expand Pollito Master into a broader personal productivity workspace.

**User value**
Users gain a more complete system for managing goals, habits, notes, and progress alongside planning.

**Main features**
- Notes
- Goals
- Habits
- Calendar import/export
- Statistics

**Dependencies**
- Stable planner foundation
- Shared product patterns for editing and viewing content
- Clear product structure for personal productivity modules

**Completion criteria**
- Users can manage planning-related productivity activities in one experience
- The platform feels cohesive across multiple modules
- Core progress and reflection features are usable and understandable

**Estimated complexity**
High

---

## Phase 4 — AI

**Goal**
Introduce intelligent assistance that improves planning speed and decision-making.

**User value**
Users receive support for organizing their time more effectively and reducing planning effort.

**Main features**
- AI assistant
- Schedule optimization
- Smart suggestions
- Natural language task creation

**Dependencies**
- Mature planning experience
- Clear product boundaries for AI assistance
- Trust, privacy, and quality expectations

**Completion criteria**
- AI features feel helpful rather than intrusive
- Suggestions are relevant and understandable
- Users can benefit from assistance without losing control of their plans

**Estimated complexity**
High

---

## Phase 5 — Android Companion

**Goal**
Extend the platform into a companion experience for everyday mobility.

**User value**
Users can stay aligned with their plans while away from the desktop, with lightweight and dependable mobile access.

**Main features**
- View planner
- Quick capture
- Reliable native alarms
- Notifications
- Offline synchronization

**Dependencies**
- Stable cross-platform data model
- Shared planning and reminder concepts
- Mobile experience design
- Reliability and notification strategy

**Completion criteria**
- The companion app supports essential daily use cases
- Core planner visibility and quick capture work reliably
- Native alarms and notifications feel dependable
- Mobile use complements the desktop experience without replacing it

**Estimated complexity**
High

---

## Phase 6 — Collaboration

**Goal**
Expand Pollito Master from a personal productivity tool into a shared coordination platform.

**User value**
Families, teams, and collaborators can coordinate schedules and responsibilities more clearly.

**Main features**
- Shared planners
- Teams
- Family scheduling
- Permissions

**Dependencies**
- Strong core planning foundation
- Shared data and access model
- Trust, privacy, and collaboration design

**Completion criteria**
- Shared planning scenarios are usable and understandable
- Different user roles and permission levels are supported clearly
- Collaboration improves coordination without creating confusion

**Estimated complexity**
High

---

Out of scope for version 1

- Full collaboration workflows
- Advanced AI automation
- Deep calendar integrations beyond basic import/export
- Large-scale multi-user administration
- Complex social or community features
- Highly specialized enterprise workflows
